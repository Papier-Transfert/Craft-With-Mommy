Top 10 Paper Christmas Crafts Kids Will Beg to Make Every Holiday Season

Published on March 25, 2026

The holidays are here, and there's nothing quite like gathering around the kitchen table with your little ones to create something magical out of paper. This collection of top 10 paper Christmas crafts is packed with festive ideas that are simple enough for tiny hands and exciting enough to become your family's new favorite holiday tradition. Grab your scissors and glue, because these projects will have your kids begging to craft all season long!

What You'll Need

Most of these projects use supplies you probably already have at home.

  • Construction paper variety pack (red, green, white, and other holiday colors)
  • Washable glue sticks
  • Kid-safe scissors
  • Markers or crayons
  • Pencil

10 Paper Christmas Craft Ideas

1. Paper Plate Christmas Tree

Turn a simple paper plate into a cheerful 3D Christmas tree that your little one can decorate however they like! Kids love this one because they get to go wild with colorful paper "ornaments," stickers, and glitter. Just fold a green-painted plate into a cone shape and let your child glue on small circles of construction paper as decorations. It's one of those easy paper Christmas crafts for kids that looks adorable on a shelf or mantel.

Age: 2+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Medium

2. Construction Paper Candy Canes

These twisty candy canes are so fun to make and look great hanging on the tree or taped to a window. Kids simply cut red and white strips of paper and twist or braid them together into a classic candy cane shape. It's a wonderful way to practice fine motor skills while creating something festive. Preschoolers especially love bending the hook at the top all by themselves!

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 3+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

3. Handprint Paper Wreath

This keepsake craft is one that families come back to year after year. Trace your child's handprints on green construction paper, cut them out, and arrange them in a circle on a paper plate ring to form a beautiful wreath. Add a red paper bow at the top and you have a gift-worthy decoration. It's a perfect project for simple Christmas paper crafts for toddlers and preschoolers because even the youngest crafters can help with tracing and gluing.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 2+ | Time: 20 min | Mess: Low

4. Paper Chain Garland

A classic that never gets old! Paper chain garlands are one of the simplest holiday crafts around, and kids of all ages can join in. Cut strips of red, green, and white paper, then loop and glue them together into a long, colorful chain. Drape it across the mantel, around the tree, or along a staircase railing. This is a great group activity when siblings or friends want to craft together during the holidays.

Age: 2+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Low

5. Paper Snowflake Ornaments

There's real magic in unfolding a piece of paper to discover the unique snowflake your child just created. Fold white paper into quarters or smaller sections, then snip shapes along the edges with kid-safe scissors. When you unfold it, every single snowflake turns out different, which kids absolutely love. Attach a string or ribbon and you have beautiful paper Christmas ornaments kids can make to hang on the tree with pride.

Age: 4+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

6. Santa Paper Bag Puppet

Bring Santa to life with a jolly paper bag puppet that your child can use for holiday storytelling and imaginative play! Use a brown paper lunch bag as the base, then add a red construction paper hat, cotton ball beard (or torn white paper strips), and googly eyes. Kids love giving their Santa a personality with silly faces and rosy cheeks drawn with markers. This craft keeps little ones entertained long after the glue dries.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 3+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Medium

7. Paper Strip Christmas Star

This gorgeous star is made by weaving and folding strips of gold or yellow construction paper into a stunning 3D shape. For younger kids, a simpler version works great: just glue five paper strips into a star outline on a piece of cardstock. Older kids (ages 5 and up) can try folding and interlocking the strips for a more dimensional look. Either way, it makes a showstopping tree topper or window decoration that your child will beam over.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 3+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Low

8. Reindeer Headband

Let your kids become Rudolph (or any of the other eight reindeer) with this wearable craft! Cut a strip of brown construction paper to fit around your child's head, then attach antlers made from traced handprints. Add a red paper nose and your little one is ready for a reindeer parade around the living room. This is one of those Christmas construction paper crafts for little hands that doubles as a costume accessory for holiday parties and school events.

Age: 3+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Low

9. Accordion-Fold Paper Angel

These sweet little angels are made by folding paper in an accordion style to create a fan-shaped body and wings. Use white or pastel paper for the body, add a circle head with a drawn-on face, and top it off with a small gold paper halo. Kids love the satisfying rhythm of folding back and forth, and the finished angel looks absolutely precious. Place them along a windowsill or tie a string through the top to create fun holiday paper crafts for kids ages 2 to 8 that the whole family can enjoy.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 4+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Low

10. Paper Gingerbread House Card

This adorable craft doubles as a handmade holiday card for grandparents, teachers, or anyone special on your child's list! Fold a piece of brown construction paper in half, cut the top into a roof shape, and let kids decorate the front with paper "candy" cutouts, drawn-on icing, and colorful dots. Open it up and there's room inside for a sweet holiday message or drawing. It's the perfect way to wrap up this list of top 10 paper Christmas crafts with a project that spreads love and cheer.

Age: 3+ | Time: 20 min | Mess: Low

Final Thoughts

We hope this list of top 10 paper Christmas crafts fills your holiday season with creativity, laughter, and plenty of sweet memories around the craft table. Remember, it doesn't have to be perfect to be meaningful. Your kids will treasure the time you spent making things together far more than any store-bought decoration. Happy crafting and happy holidays, mama!
