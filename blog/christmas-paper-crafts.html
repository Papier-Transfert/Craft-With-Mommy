25 Adorable Christmas Paper Crafts Kids Will Beg to Make Every Year

Published on March 21, 2026

There's something magical about sitting around the kitchen table with your little ones, a pile of colorful paper, and a glue stick that's already missing its cap. Christmas paper crafts are one of our favorite holiday traditions because they're simple, affordable, and create the sweetest keepsakes you'll treasure for years. Whether your kiddo is two or eight, these projects are designed to spark joy (and maybe a little glitter) without the stress.

Why Kids Love This Craft

Little hands were made for cutting, folding, tearing, and gluing, and that's exactly what easy christmas paper crafts for kids are all about. Paper is forgiving, inexpensive, and comes in every color of the rainbow, so kids get to make real creative choices without any pressure. The confidence boost they get from holding up a finished ornament or paper chain and saying "I made that!" is honestly priceless.

Beyond the fun factor, christmas paper crafts for toddlers and preschoolers build fine motor skills, color recognition, and the ability to follow simple steps. They also give you a wonderful screen-free activity during those long winter afternoons when everyone has a little too much holiday energy. Plus, handmade decorations make the tree feel extra special when Grandma comes to visit.

What You'll Need

Here's everything you'll want to have on hand before you start crafting. Most of these items work across all 25 projects, so stock up once and you're set for the whole season.

  • Christmas construction paper variety pack (red, green, white, gold) is the foundation for nearly every project on this list.
  • Washable glue sticks, at least 4 to 6 so nobody has to wait for a turn.
  • Kids' safety scissors with rounded tips work great for ages 3 and up.
  • Holiday craft sticker sheets add instant sparkle to any paper ornament or card.
  • Washable markers (10-color pack) for drawing faces, details, and holiday messages.
  • Mini pom-poms in assorted colors make perfect noses, ornaments, and snowball accents.
  • Single-hole punch for threading ribbon or yarn through finished ornaments.
  • Red and green craft ribbon for hanging your simple paper christmas ornaments for preschoolers on the tree.
  • A pencil for tracing shapes (you definitely have one in a junk drawer somewhere).
  • Paper towels or an old placemat to protect the table.

Step-by-Step Instructions

The beauty of these 25 christmas paper craft ideas for little kids is that they all follow the same basic flow. Once your child learns these five steps, they can mix and match shapes, colors, and embellishments to create everything from paper Christmas trees to reindeer faces to snowflake garlands. Here's the core process.

Step 1: Pick a Shape and Trace It

Start by choosing a simple holiday shape: a Christmas tree triangle, a circle for an ornament, a star, a candy cane, or a stocking. Draw or print a template on cardstock, then let your child trace it onto their chosen color of construction paper. For toddlers, you can pre-trace the shapes and let them take over from Step 2. Older kids (ages 5 and up) love picking their own shapes and tracing freehand, so give them the freedom to experiment.

💡 Tip: Cut a few templates from cereal box cardboard. They're sturdier than paper and little hands can hold them flat while tracing.

Step 2: Cut Out the Pieces

Let your child cut along the traced lines with their safety scissors. Remind them that wobbly edges are totally fine and actually give handmade crafts their charm. If your child is under three or still learning to use scissors, go ahead and do the cutting yourself. You can also tear the paper along the lines for a fun textured look that toddlers absolutely love.

Step 3: Decorate and Embellish

This is where the magic happens! Set out markers, stickers, pom-poms, and any other fun bits you have. Let your little one go wild decorating their cutout shapes. A green triangle becomes a Christmas tree covered in colorful "ornament" dots. A white circle turns into a snowman face with a marker smile and a pom-pom nose. There are no rules here, only creativity.

For holiday paper crafts for kids to make in a group setting (like a playdate or classroom party), try putting supplies in muffin tins so each child has easy access to everything without the inevitable "she took all the stickers" meltdown.

💡 Tip: Dot a tiny amount of white glue (instead of a glue stick) wherever you're attaching pom-poms. They'll actually stay put once it dries.

Step 4: Assemble Your Craft

Now it's time to put everything together. Depending on which of the 25 projects you're making, this could mean gluing a decorated triangle onto a brown rectangle "trunk" for a tree, layering three circles to make a snowman, or folding a paper plate in half and adding a paper Santa beard. If you're making ornaments, use the hole punch to pop a hole at the top and thread a piece of ribbon through for hanging.

For paper chains and garlands, show your child how to loop strips of paper and glue the ends together. Even three-year-olds can master this with a little help, and the pride they feel when you drape their chain across the mantel is just the best.

Step 5: Display and Admire

The final step is the most important one: celebrate what they made! Hang paper ornaments on the tree, tape paper wreaths to bedroom doors, string garlands along the staircase, or prop up handmade cards on the windowsill. When kids see their christmas paper crafts displayed front and center, it tells them their creativity matters. Snap a photo for the family group chat, too, because Grandma is going to lose it over that lopsided paper reindeer.

💡 Tip: Write your child's name and the year on the back of each craft in permanent marker. You'll be so glad you did when you unbox these treasures next December.

Variations to Try

3D Paper Christmas Trees: Instead of a flat triangle, cut three identical tree shapes, fold each one in half, and glue the halves together to create a tree that stands up on its own. Kids can decorate each side before assembling. These look amazing lined up on a bookshelf or as a centerpiece for the holiday dinner table, and they're still simple enough for preschoolers with a little guidance.

Paper Plate Wreath: Cut the center out of a paper plate to create a ring shape, then let kids glue on torn pieces of green construction paper, red pom-poms for "berries," and a ribbon bow at the top. This is one of the best christmas construction paper crafts for toddlers because tearing paper is easier than cutting and feels wonderfully satisfying for tiny hands.

Handprint Reindeer Card: Trace your child's hand on brown construction paper, cut it out, and flip it upside down so the fingers become antlers. Add googly eyes, a red pom-pom nose, and glue it to a folded card. Write a sweet message inside and mail it to someone special. It's a keepsake card and a craft all in one!

Final Thoughts

Christmas paper crafts are proof that you don't need fancy supplies or hours of prep to make beautiful holiday memories with your kids. A few sheets of construction paper, some glue, and a whole lot of love are really all it takes. So grab your supplies, put on some holiday music, and let those little hands create something wonderful. And when you're done, we'd love to see what you made! Snap a photo and share it on Pinterest so other craft-loving families can get inspired, too. Tag us or pin directly from this post so we can cheer you on. Happy crafting, friend!
