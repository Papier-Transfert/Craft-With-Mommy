13 Adorably Spooky Halloween Crafts Using Construction Paper That Kids Will Beg to Make

Published on March 24, 2026

When the leaves start turning and pumpkins pop up on every porch, it's time to break out the craft supplies! These halloween crafts using construction paper are perfect for little hands that want to get in on the spooky fun. We've rounded up 13 adorably spooky ideas that are simple enough for toddlers yet exciting enough to keep bigger kids engaged, too.

What You'll Need

Most of these projects call for the same basic supplies you probably already have at home.

  • Construction paper variety pack (black, orange, green, white, purple)
  • Washable glue sticks
  • Safety scissors for kids
  • Markers or crayons
  • Pencil

13 Adorably Spooky Halloween Craft Ideas

1. Paper Strip Pumpkin

This cheerful little pumpkin is made by cutting orange construction paper into strips and fastening them together in a round shape. Kids love watching flat strips transform into a 3D pumpkin right before their eyes! Add a green paper stem on top and you've got an instant construction paper halloween decoration for kids that looks adorable on a shelf or windowsill.

Age: 3+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

2. Silly Monster Faces

Let your little ones go wild cutting out eyes, teeth, horns, and hair to build their very own monster faces. There's no wrong way to make a monster, which makes this one of the best easy halloween construction paper crafts for toddlers. The sillier, the better! Use bold colors like purple, green, and blue for extra fun.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 2+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Low

3. Handprint Spiders

Trace your child's hands on black construction paper, cut them out, and overlap them to create an eight-legged spider. Glue on some googly eyes (or draw them with a white marker) and you've got a creepy crawly friend. Kids absolutely love seeing their own handprints turned into something spooky and fun.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 2+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

4. Paper Bag Witch

Using a triangle of black construction paper for the hat, green paper for the face, and some yarn or paper strips for hair, kids can build a friendly witch on a paper bag. This is a great project for storytelling and pretend play once it's done. Slip it over a little hand and you've got an instant puppet!

Age: 3+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Low

5. Construction Paper Bat

Fold a piece of black construction paper in half, draw a simple bat wing shape, and cut it out for a perfectly symmetrical bat. Little ones can add fangs, eyes, and even a tiny bow tie with paper scraps. Hang a few of these from the ceiling with string for spooky construction paper crafts for kids that double as decorations. This is one of those halloween crafts using construction paper that looks impressive but takes almost no time at all.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 3+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

6. Candy Corn Collage

Draw a large candy corn shape on white paper and let kids tear yellow, orange, and white construction paper into small pieces to fill in each section. Tearing paper is wonderful for building fine motor skills, and the textured result looks fantastic. This is a calming, sensory-friendly craft that even the youngest crafters can enjoy.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 2+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Medium

7. Paper Chain Ghost Garland

Cut white construction paper into strips, loop them into a chain, and add a ghost face to every few links using a black marker. This is a wonderful group activity if you have multiple kids crafting together. Drape your ghost garland across a doorway or mantel for festive, handmade Halloween decor the whole family will love.

Age: 4+ | Time: 20 min | Mess: Low

8. Pumpkin Patch Scene

Create an entire pumpkin patch on a large sheet of blue or black construction paper. Kids can cut out orange pumpkins, green vines, a yellow moon, and even a little scarecrow. This collage-style project lets kids practice their cutting and arranging skills while building a scene they can be proud of. It makes a perfect simple halloween paper craft for preschoolers when you pre-cut some of the smaller shapes.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 3+ | Time: 20 min | Mess: Low

9. Frankenstein Face

A green rectangle, some black hair strips, bolts made from tiny gray circles, and a zigzag mouth are all it takes to bring Frankenstein's monster to life. Kids love assembling his goofy face piece by piece. This is a great project for practicing shapes and following simple steps.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 3+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

10. Spiderweb Suncatcher

Fold a piece of black construction paper and cut slits into it (like making a paper snowflake) to reveal a web pattern. Glue a sheet of white tissue paper behind it and tape it to a window for a beautiful spiderweb suncatcher. When the light shines through, kids will be amazed at what they created. This one earns big "wows" for very little effort.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 5+ | Time: 15 min | Mess: Low

11. Black Cat Paper Craft

Cut out a simple black circle for the head, two pointy triangles for ears, and add green paper eyes and pipe cleaner whiskers. Glue everything onto a popsicle stick and you've got a black cat puppet ready for Halloween adventures. This is a wonderful halloween construction paper project for little kids who love animals and pretend play.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 2+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

12. Haunted House Collage

Using dark-colored construction paper, kids can build a haunted house complete with crooked windows, a spooky door, and a moon peeking out from behind the rooftop. Encourage them to add bats, ghosts, or a graveyard around the house. This open-ended project sparks so much creativity and makes a fantastic piece of halloween crafts using construction paper wall art for the fridge.

[ROUNDUP_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]

Age: 4+ | Time: 25 min | Mess: Low

13. Mummy Wrap Bookmark

Cut a bookmark shape from white construction paper, wrap it loosely with thin white paper strips (leaving gaps for googly eyes to peek through), and glue everything in place. This little mummy bookmark is a perfect take-home treat for Halloween parties or classroom celebrations. Kids love that they're making something they can actually use every time they read a book.

Age: 4+ | Time: 10 min | Mess: Low

Final Thoughts
