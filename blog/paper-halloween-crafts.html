13 Adorably Spooky Paper Halloween Crafts Your Kids Will Beg to Make Every Year

Published on March 26, 2026

When the air gets crisp and pumpkins start popping up on porches, it's the perfect time to gather your little ones around the table for some creative fun. These 13 adorably spooky paper halloween crafts are easy enough for tiny hands yet exciting enough to make your kids squeal with delight. From friendly ghosts to silly spiders, every project on this list uses simple supplies you probably already have at home.

What You'll Need

Most of these crafts call for the same basic supplies, so grab these before you get started:

  • Construction paper variety pack (black, orange, white, green, purple)
  • Washable glue sticks
  • White paper plates
  • Child-safe scissors
  • Markers or crayons

13 Adorably Spooky Paper Halloween Craft Ideas

1. Paper Plate Ghost

This friendly little ghost is one of the most beloved paper plate halloween crafts for preschoolers because it comes together in minutes. Simply cut a paper plate into a ghost shape, add googly eyes or draw silly faces, and attach tissue paper streamers at the bottom for a flowy effect. Kids adore waving their ghosts around the house and "haunting" their siblings. Hang them from the ceiling for an instant Halloween decoration the whole family will enjoy.

👶 Ages 2+ ⏱ 10 min 💧 Mess: Low

2. Construction Paper Pumpkin Patch

Let your little ones build an entire pumpkin patch right on the kitchen table! Cut orange construction paper into circles, add green stems and curly vines, and glue everything onto a big blue background. This is a wonderful project for practicing scissor skills and learning about shapes. Your toddler can even tear the paper instead of cutting for a fun textured look.

👶 Ages 2+ ⏱ 15 min 💧 Mess: Low

3. Accordion-Fold Paper Spider

There's something irresistibly fun about making a spider with wiggly accordion legs. Fold two strips of black construction paper back and forth to create bouncy legs, then attach them to a round body with a goofy face. This is one of those easy paper halloween crafts for kids that teaches patience and fine motor skills while producing the cutest creepy crawler. Stick them on walls or dangle them from doorways for extra spooky fun.

👶 Ages 3+ ⏱ 15 min 💧 Mess: Low

4. Torn Paper Candy Corn Collage

No scissors needed for this one, making it perfect for your youngest crafters. Have your toddler tear yellow, orange, and white paper into small pieces and glue them onto a candy corn template. The torn edges give it a wonderful mosaic effect that looks impressive on the fridge. This is a top pick among halloween construction paper crafts for toddlers because it keeps little hands busy and builds fine motor strength.

👶 Ages 2+ ⏱ 10 min 💧 Mess: Medium

5. Paper Bag Witch Puppet

Turn a plain brown paper bag into a cackling (but cute!) witch puppet. Add a purple or black construction paper hat, draw a friendly face, and glue on yarn hair for extra character. Kids love putting on puppet shows after they finish crafting, so don't be surprised if a whole Halloween play breaks out. This project sparks creativity and imaginative play in the best way.

👶 Ages 3+ ⏱ 20 min 💧 Mess: Low

6. Paper Plate Black Cat

Fold a paper plate in half, paint or color it black, and add pointy ears, whiskers, and bright green eyes for an adorable Halloween kitty. You can attach a curly paper tail to the back for extra charm. Little ones love giving their cats unique expressions, from silly grins to spooky stares. It's a simple halloween paper art project that doubles as a fun standing decoration for your mantel.

👶 Ages 2+ ⏱ 15 min 💧 Mess: Medium

7. Handprint Paper Bat

Trace your child's hands on black construction paper, cut them out, and turn them into bat wings attached to a small oval body. Add wiggly eyes and tiny paper fangs for a friendly (not frightening) bat buddy. Kids get a real kick out of seeing their own handprints become part of the art. Save these year after year to see how much those little hands have grown!

👶 Ages 2+ ⏱ 10 min 💧 Mess: Low

8. Paper Chain Pumpkin

Create a 3D pumpkin by looping strips of orange construction paper into a ball shape and topping it with a green paper stem. This craft introduces kids to basic engineering concepts as they figure out how to make flat paper look round. It's a satisfying project because the result looks so polished. String several together for a gorgeous paper halloween crafts garland across your fireplace.

👶 Ages 4+ ⏱ 15 min 💧 Mess: Low

9. Spooky Paper Plate Mummy

Wrap a paper plate in strips of white paper or crepe paper streamers, leaving gaps for two big googly eyes to peek through. The wrapping process is wonderfully therapeutic for kids and helps develop hand coordination. This is one of those spooky paper crafts for kids that looks impressively creepy with very little effort. Hang your mummy on the front door to greet trick-or-treaters.

👶 Ages 3+ ⏱ 15 min 💧 Mess: Low

10. Construction Paper Haunted House

Cut out a big black house shape, then let your kids go wild adding windows, doors, bats, and a spooky moon from colored paper. This open-ended project is wonderful because there's no wrong way to do it. Encourage your child to tell a story about who lives in their haunted house. It's a great way to blend crafting with storytelling and spark that Halloween imagination.

👶 Ages 4+ ⏱ 25 min 💧 Mess: Low

11. Paper Plate Frankenstein

Paint a paper plate green (or use green paper glued over it), add a flat black paper "hair" across the top, and draw on a silly stitched-up grin. Bolts made from small paper rectangles on each side complete the look. Preschoolers especially love this one because Frankenstein's monster is more goofy than scary. It's a perfect addition to your collection of paper plate halloween crafts for preschoolers.

👶 Ages 3+ ⏱ 15 min 💧 Mess: Medium

12. Paper Strip Skeleton

Use white paper strips and a round head shape on a black background to build a silly skeleton. Kids can arrange the strips however they like for arms, legs, ribs, and a spine. This project is surprisingly educational because it gets children curious about bones and how their bodies are put together. Add a big toothy grin and you've got one happy (and not at all scary) skeleton pal.

👶 Ages 3+ ⏱ 20 min 💧 Mess: Low

13. Folded Paper Jack-o'-Lantern Garland

Fold orange paper in half, cut out a half-pumpkin shape along the fold, and open it up to reveal a symmetrical jack-o'-lantern. Make a whole bunch, add faces with black marker, and string them together for a festive garland. This is a fantastic easy paper halloween crafts for kids activity for a playdate or classroom party. Your little ones will beam with pride seeing their handmade garland displayed across the living room.

👶 Ages 4+ ⏱ 20 min 💧 Mess: Low

Final Thoughts

Whether you tackle one of these paper halloween crafts today or save the whole list for a rainy October afternoon, the most important thing is the time you're spending together creating something fun. Don't worry about perfection because those wobbly ghost eyes and lopsided pumpkins are what make handmade crafts so special. Happy crafting, mama, and have the most wonderful Halloween season with your little ones!
