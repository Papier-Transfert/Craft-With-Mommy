Easy Paper Plate Rainbow Fish Craft for Toddlers (Just 5 Simple Steps!)

Published on March 17, 2026

🎨

If your little one loves bright colors and fishy fun, you're going to adore this craft! This easy paper plate rainbow fish craft for toddlers is one of our all-time favorites — it's simple enough for tiny hands, gorgeous enough to hang on the fridge, and it comes together in about 15 minutes. Grab a paper plate, some colorful supplies, and let's make something magical together!

Why Kids Love This Craft

There's something about fish that absolutely captivates toddlers and preschoolers. Maybe it's the shimmer, the scales, or the way they swoosh through the water — whatever it is, kids go wild for fish crafts! This easy rainbow fish art project for toddlers taps into that excitement by letting little ones layer on colorful tissue paper, creating their very own dazzling fish. The textures, the crinkly sounds, and the rainbow of colors make it a total sensory win.

Beyond the fun factor, this craft is packed with developmental goodness. Tearing and crumpling tissue paper strengthens those fine motor muscles. Gluing pieces onto the plate builds hand-eye coordination. And choosing colors? That's creative decision-making in action! Whether your child is 2 or 5, they'll feel so proud holding up their finished rainbow fish. It's the kind of craft that builds confidence while keeping little hands busy — and honestly, that's the sweet spot we're always looking for, right?

What You'll Need

  • 1 white paper plate (standard size works great, but small plates are perfect for little hands too)
  • Tissue paper in rainbow colors (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple)
  • White school glue or a glue stick
  • Scissors (for grown-up use)
  • 1 googly eye (or you can draw one with a marker)
  • Markers or crayons for details
  • Optional: glitter glue or metallic/holographic paper for a special "rainbow scale"
  • Optional: a small piece of cardstock for the tail fin

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep Your Paper Plate Fish Shape

Start by cutting a triangular wedge out of one side of the paper plate — this creates the fish's mouth! Don't toss that triangle, though — flip it around and glue it to the opposite side of the plate to make the tail fin. (If you prefer a sturdier tail, you can cut one from cardstock instead.) If your toddler is on the younger side, go ahead and do this step yourself. For preschoolers who are comfortable with safety scissors, let them give it a try with your guidance. The plate should now look like a cute fish silhouette — mouth on one end, tail on the other!

Step 1 🖐️

Step 2: Cut or Tear the Tissue Paper

Here's where the fun really begins! Take your rainbow-colored tissue paper and either cut it into small squares (about 1–2 inches) or — even better — let your toddler tear it into pieces. Tearing tissue paper is seriously satisfying for little ones, and it's amazing fine motor practice. Don't worry about perfect shapes at all. Crinkly, crumpled, jagged pieces are exactly what we want. Set out a few small piles of each color so your child can easily grab what they need. This is a great moment to talk about colors too — "Can you find the blue? What about orange?"

Step 2 🖐️

Step 3: Glue the Tissue Paper "Scales"

Now it's time to turn that plain paper plate into a stunning rainbow fish! Have your child spread glue onto a section of the plate (or use a glue stick if you want less mess) and press tissue paper pieces onto it. You can guide them to create rainbow rows — red at the top, then orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple toward the bottom — or just let them go free-form with a beautiful mosaic of colors. Both approaches look amazing! For a paper plate fish craft with tissue paper for preschoolers, you can show them how to scrunch each square around the eraser end of a pencil before gluing it down for a cool 3D texture effect. Toddlers will likely prefer the "stick it anywhere" method, and that is absolutely perfect.

Step 3 🖐️

Step 4: Add the Eye and Details

Once your fish is covered in gorgeous, colorful tissue paper, it's time to give it some personality! Glue a googly eye near the mouth opening. If you don't have googly eyes on hand, no worries — just draw a circle with a black marker and add a little white dot for the shine. You can also use a marker to draw a smile, add little fin lines, or outline the mouth. Want to make it extra special like the fish from the beloved storybook? Cut a small heart or scale shape from holographic or metallic paper and glue it on as the "special shiny scale." Kids absolutely light up with this detail!

Step 4 🖐️

Step 5: Let It Dry and Show It Off

Set your beautiful rainbow fish somewhere flat to dry for about 10–15 minutes (a little longer if you used lots of white glue). Once it's dry, it's time for the best part — showing it off! Tape it to the fridge, hang it on a bulletin board, or attach a piece of yarn to the back and hang it from the ceiling for an adorable "underwater" display. If you made several fish with siblings or a playgroup, you could create a whole ocean scene on a blue poster board. Your little artist is going to be so proud, and honestly, these are the kinds of crafts that make the best keepsakes.

Step 5 🖐️

Tips for Success

  • For 2-year-olds: Keep it super simple! Pre-cut all the tissue paper, use a glue stick instead of liquid glue, and skip the fancy details. A colorful paper plate fish craft for 2 year olds doesn't need to be perfect — it just needs to be fun. Let them crumple, stick, and explore at their own pace.
  • For preschoolers (ages 3–5): Challenge them a little more! Let them tear their own tissue paper, try the pencil-scrunch technique, and encourage them to create rainbow patterns or rows. This is also a great age to read The Rainbow Fish by Marcus Pfister before or after crafting.
  • Manage the mess: Lay down a plastic tablecloth or craft mat before you start. Tissue paper dye can stain surfaces when wet, so covering your table is a smart move. Keep baby wipes nearby for sticky fingers!
  • Make it a learning moment: Use this craft to practice color names, counting scales, or talking about ocean animals. You can even play ocean sounds in the background for a fun, immersive experience!

Variations to Try

Painted Rainbow Fish: If tissue paper isn't your thing, try this version instead! Let your child paint the paper plate with watercolors or tempera paint in rainbow stripes, then add the eye and details once it dries. This is a wonderful simple paper plate fish craft for kids who love getting messy with a paintbrush. You'll get the same gorgeous rainbow effect with a totally different process.

Collage-Style Fish: Raid your recycling bin and scrap drawer! Use fabric scraps, cupcake liners, sticker dots, sequins, or even pieces of aluminum foil to create the fish's scales. This mixed-media approach is fantastic for older toddlers and preschoolers who love variety. Every fish turns out completely unique, which makes it extra special.

Ocean Scene Extension: Turn your easy paper plate rainbow fish craft for toddlers into a full art project! Glue the finished fish onto a large sheet of blue construction paper, then add green tissue paper seaweed, cotton ball bubbles, and maybe even a few shell stickers. It makes for an impressive display and extends the creative fun for kids who just don't want to stop crafting.
